Why a glossary?

Some scientific terms deserve clear explanations to better understand Lise Meitner, nuclear fission, scientific ethics, and the Matilda effect. This glossary provides simple, useful definitions.

Key notions

Atom

The basic unit of matter, made of a central nucleus (protons and neutrons) and electrons moving around it.

Atomic nucleus

The central part of an atom, containing protons and neutrons. It affects stability, energy, and reactions such as fission.

Nuclear fission

A reaction where an atomic nucleus splits into two smaller nuclei, releasing energy and neutrons.

Uranium 235

An isotope of uranium capable of undergoing nuclear fission. It is used in some reactors and has historical importance in nuclear energy.

Neutron

A particle with no electric charge found in the nucleus. In fission, released neutrons can trigger a chain reaction.

Radioactivity

The property of unstable nuclei to transform spontaneously, emitting particles or radiation.

Matilda effect

A concept describing how women’s scientific contributions are often minimized or credited to men.

Scientific ethics

Reflection on researchers’ moral responsibility, especially when a discovery can be used for harmful purposes.
