Why a glossary?

Some scientific terms require clear explanations to better understand Lise Meitner, nuclear fission, scientific ethics, and the Matilda Effect. This glossary provides simple and useful definitions.

Key terms

Atom

The fundamental unit of matter, composed of a central nucleus (protons and neutrons) and electrons moving around it.

Atomic nucleus

The central part of the atom containing protons and neutrons. It plays a key role in stability and nuclear reactions.

Nuclear fission

A reaction in which an atomic nucleus splits into two smaller nuclei, releasing energy and neutrons.

Uranium-235

An isotope of uranium capable of undergoing nuclear fission, used in some reactors and historically central to nuclear energy.

Neutron

An electrically neutral particle found in the nucleus. During fission, released neutrons can trigger a chain reaction.

Radioactivity

The property of unstable nuclei to transform spontaneously, emitting particles or radiation.

Matilda Effect

A concept describing the tendency to minimize or attribute to men the scientific contributions of women.

Scientific ethics

Reflection on the moral responsibility of researchers, especially when discoveries may have dangerous applications.
