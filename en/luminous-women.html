Giving a voice back to the forgotten

Feminine Light

Published in 2024, Feminine Light gives a voice back to those whom history has too often erased. Blending biography and literary reflection, the book highlights the journeys of Marie Curie and Lise Meitner, restoring their place in scientific memory.

A narrative that repairs oblivion

Rather than static portraits, the book reveals both the strength and the fragility of these women.

Behind every discovery lies a struggle: against prejudice, against institutions, against erasure.

Writing becomes a tool of repair — a way to bring light back where textbooks too often erased.

“This book is not only about science. It is about light — the light taken away from women when their names are forgotten, and the light rekindled each time they are read.”

Excerpt — Feminine Light: Marie Curie and Women in Science, 2024

Page after page, biography becomes an act of memory — and literature, a form of justice.

When literature becomes memory

In Feminine Light, literature acts as a laboratory of remembrance. Words and stories become recognition.

Each chapter gives these researchers a voice, a trace, a place in history. The portrait of Lise Meitner, a pioneer of nuclear fission, is a striking example.

Her journey shows how science and literature can dialogue to restore a place to those history has pushed aside.

A legacy to pass on

By celebrating Marie Curie — a universal symbol of perseverance and curiosity — Feminine Light does more than honor a figure: it restores a collective legacy.

The book reminds us that knowledge is not measured only in discoveries, but also in written traces and shared memory.

Writing means resisting oblivion. It rekindles the light of those whose flame was hidden.
