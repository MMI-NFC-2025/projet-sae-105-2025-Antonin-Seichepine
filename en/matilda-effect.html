Understanding the Matilda effect

The Matilda effect describes how women’s scientific contributions are minimized, ignored, or attributed to men.

The Matilda timeline

From Hypatia to Lise Meitner, these women shaped science from the shadows. Explore the timeline following their journeys across the centuries.

  1. Hypatia of Alexandria (c. 370 - 415)

    Mathematician and astronomer of Antiquity, symbol of the earliest women scholars.

  2. Ada Lovelace (1815 - 1852)

    19th-century mathematician, recognized as the first computer programmer.

  3. Lise Meitner (1878 - 1968)

    Physicist behind the discovery of nuclear fission.

    Read her biography

  4. Rosalind Franklin (1920 - 1958)

    Biologist whose X-ray images paved the way to the DNA double-helix discovery.

  5. Donna Strickland (1959 - )

    Canadian physicist, little-known before her 2018 Nobel Prize: a symbol of delayed recognition.

About the project

Lumina is an editorial project born to preserve the memory of women in science. Conceived by an MMI student, it blends research, design, and storytelling.

Learn more

Project completed in 2025 as part of an educational exercise at the MMI department of Montbéliard.
