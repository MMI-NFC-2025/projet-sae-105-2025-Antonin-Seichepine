The Matilda Effect

It gave its name to a collective erasure: that of women whom history has erased.

View their portraits

Understanding the Matilda Effect

The Matilda Effect refers to the phenomenon by which women’s scientific contributions are minimized, ignored, or attributed to men.

The Matilda timeline

From Hypatia to Lise Meitner, these women shaped science in the shadows. Explore the timeline tracing their journeys across the centuries.

  1. Hypatia of Alexandria (c. 370 – 415)

    Mathematician and astronomer of Antiquity, a symbol of the earliest learned women.

  2. Ada Lovelace (1815 – 1852)

    19th-century mathematician, the world’s first computer programmer.

  3. Lise Meitner (1878 – 1968)

    Physicist at the origin of nuclear fission.

  4. Rosalind Franklin (1920 – 1958)

    Biologist whose X-ray images enabled the discovery of DNA’s structure.

  5. Donna Strickland (1959 – )

    Canadian physicist, largely unknown to the public before her 2018 Nobel Prize: a symbol of recognition that still comes too late.

About the project

Lumina is an editorial project created to preserve the memory of women in science. Designed by an MMI student, it combines research, design, and storytelling.
