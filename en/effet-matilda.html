The Matilda Effect

It gave a name to a collective erasure: the women history pushed into the background.

See their portraits ▼

Understanding the Matilda effect

Defined by sociologist Margaret Rossiter, the Matilda effect echoes Matilda Joslyn Gage, a 19th-century activist. It describes the systematic erasure of women from scientific history.

The Matilda timeline

From Hypatia to Lise Meitner, these women shaped science in the shadows. Explore a timeline of their journeys across centuries.

  1. Hypatia of Alexandria

    c. 370 – 415

    Mathematician and astronomer in Antiquity, symbol of early women scholars.

  2. Ada Lovelace

    1815 – 1852

    Mathematician and computing pioneer, often credited as the first programmer.

  3. Lise Meitner

    1878 – 1968

    Physicist behind nuclear fission theory, long downplayed and overlooked.

  4. Rosalind Franklin

    1920 – 1958

    Her X-ray images were crucial to understanding DNA’s structure.

  5. Donna Strickland

    1959 –

    Canadian physicist, Nobel Prize in Physics (2018), a reminder that visibility still matters.
